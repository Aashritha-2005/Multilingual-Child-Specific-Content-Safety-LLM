### SECTION 5 — LABEL DEFINITIONS  

#### 5a. stereotype  
**Definition:** A stereotype is a simplified and often offensive generalization about a group of people. It reduces complex individuals to oversimplified or inaccurate beliefs about their characteristics, behaviors, or abilities.  

**Linguistic markers:**  
- Use of generalizing phrases like "all X are Y" or "X people are always Y."  
- Terms that reduce a group to a single trait (e.g., "women are emotional").  

**tel-specific cues:**  
- Phrases like "అన్ని X లోపాలు" (all X have flaws).  
- Terms like "మహిళలు ఎల్లప్పుడూ భావుకులు" (women are always emotional).  

**Borderline cases:**  
- Statements that are true but non-offensive generalizations (e.g., "many people like coffee").  
- Statements that critique a group's actions without generalizing to all members.  

---

#### 5b. vilification  
**Definition:** Vilification involves extreme and disrespectful language that severely demeans or defames a person or group. It often includes hateful or inflammatory speech intended to harm someone's reputation or well-being.  

**Linguistic markers:**  
- Use of slurs, curses, or highly offensive terms.  
- Calls for violence, exclusion, or punishment.  

**tel-specific cues:**  
- Terms like "దుర్మార్గుడు" ( wicked person), "నిర్దాక్షిణ్యుడు" (gullible person).  
- Phrases like "ఇతన్ని చంపేయాలి" (he should be killed).  

**Borderline cases:**  
- Strong criticism that is not paired with hateful language or calls for harm.  
- Blunt but non-hateful language used in arguments.  

---

#### 5c. dehumanization  
**Definition:** Dehumanization involves language that strips individuals or groups of their humanity, often by comparing them to animals, objects, or lesser beings.  

**Linguistic markers:**  
- Comparisons to animals (e.g., "rats," "dogs").  
- Reduction of people to inanimate objects or less than human.  

**tel-specific cues:**  
- Terms like "జంతువుల వలె ప్రవర్తిస్తారు" (acting like animals).  
- Phrases like "ఇవరు మానవులు కాదు" (they are not humans).  

**Borderline cases:**  
- Metaphorical comparisons that are not offensive (e.g., "he runs like a cheetah").  
- Non-dehumanizing insults that do not reduce people to lesser beings.  

---

#### 5d. extreme_language  
**Definition:** Extreme language involves highly charged or aggressive speech that goes beyond ordinary profanity or insult. It often includes threats, graphic descriptions, or deeply offensive terms.  

**Linguistic markers:**  
- Explicit threats or wishes for harm.  
- Graphic or disturbing descriptions of violence.  

**tel-specific cues:**  
- Terms like "నిన్ను చంపుతా" (I will kill you).  
- Phrases like "ఇతని మొహం పగుల కొట్టు" (break his face).  

**Borderline cases:**  
- Ordinary profanity or insults without threats or graphic content.  
- Hyperbolic language used in non-serious contexts (e.g., "I hate this so much").  

---

#### 5e. lack_of_empathy  
**Definition:** Lack of empathy involves language that shows disregard for others' feelings, suffering, or rights. It often manifests as dismissive, uncaring, or unfeeling responses to sensitive issues.  

**Linguistic markers:**  
- Dismissive phrases like "who cares?" or "not my problem."  
- Statements minimizing harm or suffering.  

**tel-specific cues:**  
- Terms like "ఎవరికెవరు పట్టింపు లేదు" (nobody cares).  
- Phrases like "ఏమైనా జరిగిపోయింది, ఇది పెద్ద లోపం కాదు" (whatever happened, it's no big deal).  

**Borderline cases:**  
- Blunt but factual statements without emotional tone.  
- Statements expressing personal opinions without causing harm.  

---

#### 5f. invalidation  
**Definition:** Invalidation involves language that dismisses, denies, or minimizes the validity of someone’s experiences, emotions, or rights. It often undermines their legitimacy or credibility.  

**Linguistic markers:**  
- Phrases like "that never happened" or "you're overreacting."  
- Statements denying someone's right to feel or express emotions.  

**tel-specific cues:**  
- Terms like "అది నీకు జరిగినట్టు అనిపించడం వల్ల మాత్రం నిజం కాదు" (just because it feels true to you doesn’t make it true).  
- Phrases like "నీవు ఎక్కువైనా భావిస్తున్నావ్" (you're overreacting).  

**Borderline cases:**  
- Fact-based corrections without dismissive tone.  
- Statements offering alternative perspectives without undermining the speaker.
